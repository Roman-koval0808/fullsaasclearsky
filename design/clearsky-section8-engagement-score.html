Section 8 of 8 — Foundation
Engagement Score
The Engagement Score is a real-time number between 0 and 100 that reflects where a visitor is in the customer journey at this specific moment. It is deterministic — the same inputs always produce the same score. It is not a machine learning prediction. It is a truthful measurement. The bucket follows the score. Never the other way around.
Dual Score Fields — score_raw and score_live
Two fields replace the previous single score field. They serve completely different purposes and are never combined.
score_raw — The Audit Trail
63
Cumulative score at last event. Never decays. Represents everything the visitor has done — the permanent record. The audit trail the team can inspect to understand how a score was reached. score_raw only increases. It is not what the Orchestrator acts on.
Increases on every positive event. Floor: 0. Ceiling: 100.
score_live — What the Orchestrator Reads
63
score_raw multiplied by the decay factor, calculated at read time. This is what the Orchestrator acts on. When a visitor is idle, score_live falls even though score_raw stays frozen. Calculated lazily — not a background process. Fresh on every profile read.
Currently in grace period — no decay applied yet.

Negative Delta Events
Certain visitor behaviours reduce the engagement score immediately. Two types are distinguished. Score floor is zero — the score cannot go negative.
Two Types of Negative Signal
Friction signals indicate the visitor is still trying but something is in the way. Reduce score and trigger a friction intervention. Disengagement signals indicate the relationship is weakening — reduce score more aggressively and flag for reactivation.
⚠ Friction — visitor is stuck · rage click · dead click · form abandon · exit under 8s
✗ Disengagement — relationship cooling · no-show · unsubscribe · review ignored
