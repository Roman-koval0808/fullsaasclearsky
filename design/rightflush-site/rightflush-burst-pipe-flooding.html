While we're on the way

What to do right now.

Steps to protect your home in the next 15 minutes while a plumber is dispatched.

01
Do this first
Shut off the main water valve

The main shutoff is usually in the basement near the foundation wall or where the water line enters the house. Turn it clockwise until it stops. This cuts water to the entire home and stops active flooding immediately.

02
Turn off the hot water tank

Gas heater: turn the dial to Pilot. Electric: switch off the breaker marked Water Heater. Prevents the tank from running dry and burning out. Takes 30 seconds and saves a costly repair.

03
Get valuables off the floor

Documents, electronics, furniture, stored boxes — move them up or out of the affected area. Water spreads faster than it looks. Five minutes of moving things now saves hours of dealing with water damage later.

04
We're on our way

A licensed plumber is dispatched the moment you call. They'll confirm their ETA and call when they're 5 minutes out. Keep your phone close. If the situation changes while we're en route, call back immediately.

While you wait

Limiting water damage
in the first 15 minutes.

Soak up standing water fast

Towels, mops, and anything absorbent. The longer water sits on hardwood or drywall, the more damage it does. Even 10 minutes of effort now reduces the restoration bill significantly.

Open windows if it's warm out

Air circulation slows mold growth, which can start within 24–48 hours of a water event. In winter, keep the house warm — don't leave windows open if it's below zero.

Document everything

Take photos and video before cleanup — of the source, the water spread, and affected materials. Your insurance company will need this evidence. Start a claim if damage is significant.

Don't use electrical appliances

If water is near electrical outlets, junction boxes, or your electrical panel — do not touch anything. If you're concerned about safety, switch off the main breaker and stay clear until the plumber arrives.

Don't use drain cleaners

Chemical drain products can make blockages worse and damage pipes when the issue is a burst or disconnected joint. Leave the pipes alone — we'll diagnose and fix it correctly.

Find your insurance documents

Locate your home insurance policy number and the claims phone number while you wait. Significant water damage — especially from a burst pipe or sewer backup — is usually covered. Start the claim as soon as it's safe to.
