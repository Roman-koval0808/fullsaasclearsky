# **The Correct Frame for This Plan**

White glove at 30 clients per rep is sustainable. White glove at 300 clients requires 10 reps. The path to scale is not pushing work onto clients — it is automating the work the rep currently does manually so that one rep can handle more clients at the same quality level within the same 4-hour monthly allocation.

Every task the AI platform can do reliably without human review is time returned to the rep for relationship work, configuration judgment, and the things a human genuinely needs to do. The transition plan defines which tasks move to AI automation and when, and which tasks stay human permanently.

# **Phase 1 — Launch Through Month 4**

Rep load: 30 clients. Monthly allocation: 4 hours per client. Human review required on everything because the AI quality baseline is not yet established.

| **Task** | **Phase 1 approach** | **Target time per client** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Content generation and publishing | AI generates. Rep reviews every piece before publishing. | 60–90 min |
| Social media | AI drafts. Rep reviews and schedules. | 25–30 min |
| GBP review responses | AI drafts. Rep reviews before publishing. | 15–20 min |
| Marketing automation monitoring | Rep monitors event stream and bucket distribution. Adjusts config as needed. | 15–20 min |
| Monthly report | Platform generates. Rep reviews and sends. | 15–20 min |
| Client appointments | Booked via scheduling system. 15-min minimum. | 15–30 min |
| Buffer | Ad hoc requests, anomaly resolution. | 15–20 min |

Phase 1 total: 3.5–4 hours per client per month. This is the full allocation and there is almost no slack. AI automation is what prevents this from exceeding 4 hours even in Phase 1 — the rep is reviewing, not creating.

# **Phase 2 — Month 4 Through Month 8 — Automation Replaces Review**

The trigger to remove human review from any task is the same for every task: three consecutive months with zero corrections required on AI output for that client on that task. When that threshold is met, the task moves to auto-publish. The rep shifts from reviewing the output to reviewing the performance.

| **Task** | **Automation trigger** | **Time saved per client** | **Rep’s new role** |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Content auto-publish | 3 months zero corrections per client | 45–60 min | Review traffic and ranking performance. Not the content itself. |
| GBP review auto-response | 3 months zero corrections per client | 10–15 min | Monitor sentiment trends. Flag unusual patterns. |
| Social media auto-schedule | Content pipeline is autonomous first — social is downstream | 20–25 min | Monitor engagement. Flag underperforming content. |
| Report auto-delivery | Report template stable. Data connections reliable. | 15–20 min | Review reports for anomalies. Reach out proactively if numbers are off. |

Phase 2 target: 2–2.5 hours per client per month once full automation is live. This creates meaningful headroom within the 4-hour allocation for relationship work and higher-judgment tasks.

## **What the rep does with the recovered time in Phase 2**

- Proactive performance monitoring across all 30 clients — looking for patterns, not approving individual pieces

- Proactive outreach when metrics shift: audience milestone reached, ContentRadar signal about to spike, retargeting CTR dropping

- Configuration judgment: intent rules adjustments, URL pattern updates, engagement threshold calibration

- Client relationship: the rep has more time to be genuinely useful in appointment conversations because they are not spending all their time on production tasks

# **Phase 3 — Month 8 and Beyond — Exception-Based Management**

In Phase 1 and 2 the rep touches every client every month regardless of whether the client needs attention. In Phase 3 the platform identifies which clients need human attention and the rep focuses there. A client whose metrics are on track, content is publishing correctly, audiences are growing, and who has not submitted a request gets a lighter touch. A client whose traffic dropped, OAuth token failed, or ContentRadar shows a missed window gets immediate human attention.

The 4-hour monthly allocation becomes an average across the book, not a fixed per-client commitment. Some clients consume 1 hour. Some consume 6. The total across 30 clients stays within 120 hours per month.

## **What this means for rep capacity**

| **Phase** | **Client load per rep** | **Hours per client per month** | **Total hours per month** |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Phase 1 — launch | 30 | 3.5–4 | 105–120 |
| Phase 2 — automation | 30 | 2–2.5 | 60–75 |
| Phase 3 — exception-based | 30–40 | Average 2, range 1–6 | 60–120 depending on client mix |

# **What Stays Human Permanently**

Three things never get automated regardless of platform maturity:

## **1. The onboarding walkthrough call**

A contractor who just signed up for ClearSky needs a human conversation to understand what has been built for their business, what to expect, and who their rep is. This call sets the tone for the entire relationship. It stays human.

## **2. Escalations involving data, privacy, or dissatisfaction**

Any question about how visitor data is collected or used goes to Rory immediately. Any client expressing intent to cancel is a relationship conversation that goes to Rory. No automation touches these situations.

## **3. Configuration judgment calls**

Deciding whether a client’s Emergency bucket is misconfigured or whether their traffic is genuinely low-urgency requires a human who understands the client’s business. Adjusting intent rules, calibrating engagement thresholds, and interpreting bucket distribution anomalies stay with the rep permanently.

# **Transition Triggers — What Causes Each Phase Shift**

## **Phase 1 to Phase 2 — platform readiness, not a calendar date**

- AI content quality baseline established for at least 20 active clients — 3 consecutive months of zero corrections per client

- GBP review response quality baseline established — same threshold

- Monthly report template stable and data connections reliable across all active clients

- Onboarding runbook validated through at least 10 onboardings without Rory present

## **Phase 2 to Phase 3 — support load, not a calendar date**

- Average rep consistently delivering full client scope in under 2.5 hours per client per month

- At this point the rep-to-client ratio can be adjusted or the freed capacity redirected to onboarding new clients faster

| *The headcount does not scale linearly with clients. The automation investment in Phase 2 is what makes Phase 3 possible without proportional growth in rep headcount. At 100 clients ClearSky needs approximately 3–4 reps. At Phase 3 with exception-based management, the same reps can handle 120–150 clients at the same quality level.* |
