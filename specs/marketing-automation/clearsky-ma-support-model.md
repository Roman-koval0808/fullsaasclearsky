# **The Model in One Paragraph**

Each rep manages 30 clients. Each client receives 4 hours of managed marketing per month. AI does the generation and automation. The rep reviews, approves, and publishes. The rep handles client communication, configuration adjustments, and performance monitoring. When a client wants to talk, they book a 15-minute appointment slot through the ClearSky scheduling system. That time comes directly out of their 4-hour allocation. Everything else the rep does on behalf of the client — content, social, SEO, reputation, reporting, automation maintenance — is included in the same allocation.

# **The Appointment System**

Clients book appointments through the ClearSky scheduling system. Minimum slot is 15 minutes. Each appointment is booked against the client’s assigned rep. The scheduling system is the only way to get live time with the rep — unscheduled calls are redirected to book an appointment.

- 15-minute slots are the minimum unit. A client who needs 30 minutes books two slots.

- Appointment time is deducted from the client’s 4-hour monthly allocation at the time of booking.

- A client who books two 15-minute appointments in a month has used 30 minutes of their allocation before any management work is done.

- The rep uses the appointment to understand what the client needs, not to do the work live. Work is completed after the appointment within the remaining allocation.

- If a client’s appointment requests would exceed their 4-hour allocation, the rep flags this before the booking is confirmed and discusses priorities with the client.

## **Why the appointment system matters at 30 clients per rep**

Without appointment management, a rep with 30 clients who each make two unscheduled calls per month is fielding 60 interruptions. That destroys the focused work time required to manage the allocation correctly. The appointment system is not a barrier — it is what allows the rep to act as a genuine marketing manager rather than a reactive helpdesk.

# **What the Rep Does on Behalf of the Client**

The rep acts as the client’s marketing manager. The client never needs to log in, configure anything, or manage any part of the platform. The rep handles everything.

## **Included in the 4 hours**

- All content publishing — blog, FAQ, AI SEO content generated by ContentRadar and approved by the rep

- All social media — posts drafted by AI, reviewed and scheduled by the rep

- GBP management — review responses, profile updates, photo management

- Reputation monitoring — review velocity, sentiment, unanswered mentions

- Marketing automation maintenance — intent rules, audience push, event stream monitoring

- Monthly report — generated by platform, reviewed and sent by rep

- Client appointments — 15-minute minimum, time deducted from allocation

- Ad hoc requests from the client — handled within remaining allocation

## **Not included — outside scope**

- Ad creative production — ClearSky manages audiences and intelligence. Ad creative and spend are the client’s responsibility or their agency’s.

- Ad campaign management — ClearSky pushes audiences to the ad account. Campaign setup, bidding, and creative decisions are not ClearSky’s scope.

- Website development or design changes outside the ClearSky platform

- CRM or job management software support

- Anything that requires more than 4 hours per month — escalate to Rory to discuss a different service tier

# **Escalation Triggers**

Four conditions escalate to Rory immediately regardless of where they arise in the client relationship:

- Any system issue the rep cannot resolve within 48 hours — pixel failure, OAuth breakdown, hub not receiving events

- Any data privacy question or concern raised by the client — goes to Rory immediately, no exceptions

- Any client who expresses dissatisfaction or intent to cancel — relationship conversation, not a support ticket

- Any client whose 90-day metrics are below target with a clean internal health layer — retention risk

Platform bugs or anomalies that cannot be resolved through configuration escalate to the build team. The rep notifies the client that an issue has been identified and is being resolved — without technical detail. Client never hears ‘bug’ or ‘error’.

# **Proactive Monitoring — What the Rep Watches Without Being Asked**

The rep does not wait for the client to report a problem. The platform flags anomalies and the rep resolves them as part of the monthly allocation.

| **Trigger** | **Rep action** | **Timeline** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Pixel silent for 24 hours | Investigate and resolve. Do not wait for 48-hour threshold. | Same day |
| OAuth token approaching expiry | Refresh before it fails silently. | Before expiry |
| Audience membership drops 30%+ week over week | Investigate cause. Resolve or escalate. | Within 48 hours |
| ContentRadar publishing window missed | Identify cause. Escalate to content team. | Same day |
| Client has not opened monthly report two months running | Note in client record. Address on next appointment or in report intro. | Next report cycle |
