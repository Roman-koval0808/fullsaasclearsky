# **Section 1 -- What ContentRadar Is**

ContentRadar is ClearSky's proprietary watch market intelligence system. It is the only component of the entire ClearSky platform that no competitor can replicate -- because it is built on data that does not exist anywhere else.

Every other part of the diagnostic and content platform uses publicly available APIs. Google Places, Lighthouse, DataForSEO, Facebook, Firecrawl -- any developer with a credit card can access those. ContentRadar's demand-side intelligence cannot be bought, licensed, or scraped from any external source.

| ContentRadar is simultaneously: (1) a client-facing content intelligence and publishing tool, (2) the demand-side engine that powers the diagnostic's content gap calculation, and (3) the keyword library that drives Blog, FAQs, AI SEO, and the Local Ranking Tool across all platform modules. |

## **The Core Problem ContentRadar Solves**

Small trades businesses in rural Canada have no way to know what their customers are going to be searching for before they search for it. They react -- posting content about last winter's frozen pipe problem in March, publishing a sump pump article in July after the spring rush is over. The content arrives after the window closes.

ContentRadar inverts this. By monitoring 1,000 businesses in watch markets that sit geographically and economically ahead of each client's market, ContentRadar identifies what is spiking in demand four to six weeks before that demand arrives at the client's door. That window is the publishing window -- enough time to get content live and indexed before the prospect is searching.

| The result: ClearSky clients appear in search results for exactly the right keywords at exactly the right moment -- weeks before their competitors have even noticed the demand is coming. |

# **Section 2 -- The Watch Market Mechanism**

ContentRadar monitors 1,000 businesses in watch markets -- geographic and economic markets that sit ahead of each client's market in terms of search behaviour. The core insight is that demand for trades services follows predictable patterns, and those patterns show up in one market before they arrive in another.

## **How Watch Markets Are Selected**

Watch markets are selected based on three criteria applied to each client's specific market.

| **Geographic proximity** | Markets within 400-800km that share similar weather patterns and seasonal profiles. A market experiencing an extreme cold snap will see frozen pipe searches spike weeks before a neighbouring market does. |
| --- | --- |
| **Economic similarity** | Markets with comparable population size, housing stock age, and income profile. A market with similar housing age will generate similar maintenance demand sequences. |
| **Temporal lead** | Markets where search behaviour demonstrably leads the client's market by 3-8 weeks. Validated by historical keyword volume data across the ContentRadar monitoring period. |

For a Timmins plumber, watch markets would include Sudbury, Sault Ste. Marie, Thunder Bay, and comparable Northern Ontario communities where demand signals arrive ahead of Timmins. For each client, ContentRadar maintains a specific watch market set calibrated to their trade vertical and geography.

## **What Is Monitored in Watch Markets**

ContentRadar monitors search behaviour across 1,000 businesses in the watch market set. For each business in the watch market, the system tracks keyword engagement signals -- which searches are leading to clicks, bookings, calls, and content engagement for similar trade businesses in those markets.

This produces a continuously updated keyword demand signal for each trade vertical in each region. When a keyword starts spiking in the watch markets -- when frozen pipe searches jump 300% in Sudbury -- ContentRadar flags it for all Timmins clients in the plumbing vertical with sufficient lead time to publish content before the spike arrives locally.

# **Section 3 -- The Three Signal Types**

Every keyword monitored by ContentRadar is classified into one of three signal types based on the mechanism driving its demand. The signal type determines how ContentRadar alerts the client and how far in advance.

| **Signal Type 1** | **Geo-Lag -- Seasonal and Weather-Driven Demand** |
| --- | --- |

Geo-lag signals are the most common signal type. They track seasonal or weather-driven demand that travels predictably from one market to the next based on geography and climate.

| **Lead time** | 4-6 weeks ahead of local market |
| --- | --- |
| **Trigger mechanism** | Weather events, seasonal transitions, temperature thresholds |
| **Example** | A cold snap hits Sudbury in late October. Frozen pipe and burst pipe searches spike. Timmins is 4-6 weeks behind the same weather pattern. ContentRadar flags frozen pipe keywords as Critical for Timmins plumbing clients immediately. |
| **Content window** | Client needs to publish content on the flagged keyword within 5-7 days to allow indexing time before local demand peaks |
| **Validation** | Historical weather and search correlation data across multiple seasonal cycles confirms the geo-lag pattern per keyword per market pair |

| **Signal Type 2** | **Regulatory and Economic Lag -- Policy and Economic Drivers** |
| --- | --- |

Regulatory and economic lag signals track changes in building codes, government incentive programmes, insurance requirements, or economic conditions that drive service demand. These signals travel through markets in a predictable sequence as awareness and compliance requirements spread.

| **Lead time** | 2-8 weeks ahead of local market |
| --- | --- |
| **Trigger mechanism** | New building codes, government rebate programmes, insurance requirement changes, bylaw enforcement |
| **Example** | Ontario announces a new requirement for backflow preventer testing on commercial properties. Urban markets (Toronto, Ottawa) see immediate search spikes for "backflow preventer test required." Northern Ontario markets like Timmins follow 4-6 weeks later as the requirement becomes locally understood. |
| **Verticals most affected** | Electrical (code changes), HVAC (efficiency rebates, refrigerant regulations), plumbing (backflow, water quality) |
| **Tourism application** | Provincial park policy changes, fishing regulation updates, hunting season adjustments -- all generate search demand that travels from urban awareness markets to rural tourism markets |

| **Signal Type 3** | **Life Event Triggers -- Life-Stage Driven Search Demand** |
| --- | --- |

Life event trigger signals track search behaviour associated with predictable life stages that correlate with high-intent trades and professional services searches. Unlike weather or regulation, these are driven by population-level life events that generate consistent, forecastable demand.

| **Lead time** | Variable -- 2-12 weeks depending on event type |
| --- | --- |
| **Trigger mechanism** | Home purchase closing dates, insurance renewal cycles, renovation season, seasonal preparation |
| **Example (plumbing)** | Home purchase volume spikes in spring. Drain camera inspections, pre-purchase plumbing assessments, and first-year maintenance searches spike 6-8 weeks after the purchase peak as new owners discover issues. |
| **Example (professional services)** | Tax season drives estate planning and business structure searches. Divorce searches peak in January (post-holiday) and September (back to school). Wills and powers of attorney spike following major public health events. |
| **Example (tourism)** | Summer booking searches begin in February-March for Northern Ontario lodges. ContentRadar flags which package types and fishing species are spiking in southern Ontario search (the primary source market) weeks before booking intent peaks. |

# **Section 4 -- The Four Keyword States**

Every keyword in the ContentRadar monitoring database exists in one of four states at any given time. The state determines what action ContentRadar recommends and how urgently.

| **State** | **Definition** | **Client Action** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Spiking | Search volume 200% or more above the 90-day baseline in watch markets. Demand is arriving now. The publishing window is open and closing fast. | Publish now. This is a Critical gap. Content must be live within 5-7 days to capture the demand wave before competitors respond. |
| Rising | Search volume 50-200% above the 90-day baseline in watch markets. Demand is building. The publishing window is opening. | Draft and schedule. This is a High priority gap. Content should be published within 2-3 weeks to be indexed before the spike arrives locally. |
| Evergreen | Consistent search volume year-round. Not trending up or down. These are the foundational keywords for the vertical. | Already published or missing. If missing from the client website, this is a persistent Standard gap -- content should be published as part of the baseline content programme. |
| Dormant | Search volume below baseline and declining. The window has closed or has not yet opened for this keyword. | No action required. Monitor for reactivation in next cycle. Do not spend content resources on dormant keywords. |

The keyword state system ensures that ClearSky's content publishing resources are always allocated to the highest-value opportunities -- those where a specific, measurable demand wave is approaching the client's market with sufficient lead time to act.

# **Section 5 -- What ContentRadar Produces for Each Client**

ContentRadar generates four distinct outputs for each client. These outputs drive the content strategy across multiple ClearSky platform modules simultaneously.

## **Output 1 -- The Prioritised Content Calendar**

A rolling 90-day content calendar that shows, for each client, which keywords should be published when, ranked by urgency and revenue value. The calendar is updated weekly as keyword states change in the watch markets.

| **Calendar horizon** | 90 days rolling -- always 90 days ahead, updated weekly |
| --- | --- |
| **Priority ranking** | Critical (spiking keywords) > High (rising keywords) > Standard (evergreen gaps) > Maintenance (existing content refresh) |
| **Revenue weighting** | Keywords are ranked within each priority tier by estimated revenue value: monthly searches x position-1 CTR x conversion rate x average job value |
| **Publishing assignments** | Each calendar entry specifies which ClearSky module handles the content: Blog (long-form article), FAQs (question-answer format), AI SEO (structured data optimised content), or Social (Facebook/Instagram post) |

## **Output 2 -- The Content Gap Report**

A current-state analysis of the client's website versus the ContentRadar demand database. Shows which of the top 30 keywords for their vertical and market their website currently covers, which are weakly covered, and which are entirely missing.

This is the input to the diagnostic Layer 4 calculation. The supply side comes from the website scrape. The demand side comes from ContentRadar. The gap between the two is expressed as a dollar value using the content gap revenue formula.

| **Content Gap Revenue Formula** Content_Gap = SUM (for each missing or weak keyword):   Monthly_Searches x CTR_Position_1 x Conversion_Rate x Avg_Sale_Value  Applied annually: x 12 x Seasonal_Multiplier Conversion rate: 3.5% organic traffic to lead (WebFX 2026 Home Services Benchmarks) CTR Position 1: 44% of searches click Map Pack position 1 (BrightLocal) |
| --- |

Each missing keyword in the report is classified by urgency (Spiking, Rising, Evergreen) so the prospect can see not just what they are missing but what they are missing right now versus what they are persistently missing versus what is about to become urgent.

## **Output 3 -- The Keyword State Dashboard**

A live view for each client showing the current state of all monitored keywords for their vertical and market. Available to the ClearSky account management team and, in simplified form, to the client through the native app.

| **View 1 -- Spiking now** | All keywords currently at 200%+ baseline in watch markets. These require immediate content action. Sorted by revenue value. |
| --- | --- |
| **View 2 -- Rising soon** | All keywords currently 50-200% above baseline. These require content scheduling within 2-3 weeks. Sorted by projected peak date. |
| **View 3 -- Coverage status** | All evergreen keywords for the vertical -- which the client covers, which they cover weakly, which they are missing entirely. Sorted by search volume. |
| **View 4 -- Competitor presence** | For each spiking or rising keyword, which competitors in the client's market have content covering it and what their content quality score is. Identifies where the client can displace competitors. |

ContentRadar feeds structured briefs to four ClearSky platform modules automatically. Each brief contains the keyword, the signal type, the urgency level, the recommended content format, and the revenue value of the gap.

# **Section 6 -- How ContentRadar Feeds the Diagnostic**

ContentRadar is the demand-side intelligence that makes Layer 4 of the Digital Health Diagnostic possible. Without it, the diagnostic can measure what the website says but not what customers are searching for. With it, the gap becomes precise, personalised, and defensible.

## **The Layer 4 Content Gap Process**

- The prospect enters their business name, address, and website URL.

- Firecrawl scrapes the website -- extracting all visible text, headings, meta titles, meta descriptions, and FAQ content from all pages.

- The scraper output is passed to Claude API for keyword extraction -- producing the "supply footprint": the topics and phrases the website currently covers.

- ContentRadar provides the demand database: the top 30 keywords for the client's specific trade vertical in their specific market, each with its current state (Spiking, Rising, Evergreen, Dormant) and estimated monthly search volume.

- The diagnostic engine compares the supply footprint against the demand database. Each keyword is classified as Covered, Weak (appears once, no heading structure), or Missing.

- Missing and Weak keywords are scored by urgency based on their ContentRadar state. Spiking = Critical. Rising = High. Evergreen = Standard.

- Each Critical and High gap is translated to a revenue value using the content gap formula. The sum of all gap values is the Layer 4 revenue contribution to the total diagnostic gap.

| A concrete example: A Timmins plumber's website mentions water heaters but says nothing about anode rods. ContentRadar shows "anode rod replacement" is evergreen at 35 searches per month and "water heater smell rotten eggs" is currently spiking at 280% above baseline in the Sudbury watch market. The diagnostic shows both as gaps -- one Standard, one Critical -- each with a dollar value. The prospect can see exactly what they are missing and why it matters right now. |

## **The People Also Ask Layer**

ContentRadar also feeds the PAA (People Also Ask) analysis in the diagnostic. Google's PAA boxes reveal exactly what users want to know at the point of a trade search. ContentRadar's SERP API integration pulls the top 10 PAA questions for each of the client's 5 primary trade keywords.

If those questions appear in Google's PAA but not in the prospect's website FAQ section, that is a zero-to-one gap. Not just a weak signal but a complete absence of content answering a question Google is actively surfacing to searchers. Each unanswered PAA question is a competitor's opportunity.

| **PAA data source** | SerpAPI or DataForSEO SERP API -- Google search with PAA extraction enabled |
| --- | --- |
| **Match process** | Each PAA question is matched against the scraped website FAQ content. Matched = Covered. Not matched = Missing PAA gap. |
| **Revenue attribution** | Each missing PAA question represents a search result where a competitor who does answer it gets the click. Revenue attributed at: estimated searches x position 1 CTR x conversion rate x job value. |
| **FAQ module connection** | Missing PAA questions feed directly to the FAQ module which publishes the question-answer pair as structured FAQ content. This creates a direct pipeline from watch market demand signal to published FAQ answer to SERP visibility. |

# **Section 7 -- The Manufacturing Variant**

For the manufacturing vertical, ContentRadar operates differently. Instead of monitoring consumer search demand for trades services, it monitors 1,000 prospect businesses in adjacent markets for buying signals -- indicators that a business is about to make a purchasing decision for manufactured goods or components.

This is Phase 2 of the three-phase manufacturing model and represents the most sophisticated application of the watch market concept.

## **Phase 2 -- Know Your Customer**

ContentRadar monitors 1,000 downstream businesses -- the businesses that buy from ClearSky's manufacturing clients -- and builds a scored prospect database ranked by buying signal strength.

| **What is monitored** | Capital expenditure announcements, expansion news, regulatory filings, equipment tender postings, hiring patterns that indicate growth or transition, facility permit applications |
| --- | --- |
| **Signal sources** | Public regulatory filings, government tender databases, LinkedIn company updates, local business news, chamber of commerce announcements, building permits |
| **Scoring algorithm** | Each monitored business receives a Buying Signal Score (0-100) based on the strength and recency of purchase-intent signals. Score decays over time if no new signals appear. |
| **Output** | A ranked prospect database updated weekly. The top-scored businesses in the watch market are the highest-priority outreach targets for the manufacturing client in the upcoming sales cycle. |
| **Phase 3 connection** | The top-ranked prospects from Phase 2 are the audience for Phase 3 -- the Viewroom virtual trade show and the Buy Direct campaign. |

This is fundamentally different from the trades watch market mechanism. In trades, ContentRadar monitors what consumers are searching for. In manufacturing, ContentRadar monitors what businesses are signalling -- the operational and strategic indicators that precede a purchasing decision.

| The manufacturing variant transforms ContentRadar from a search demand intelligence tool into a B2B prospect intelligence tool. The mechanism is different but the core principle is the same: know what your customer is about to do before they do it. |

# **Section 10 -- Why ContentRadar Is Defensible**

ContentRadar's competitive defensibility rests on four pillars. Understanding these is important for sales conversations, investor briefings, and competitive analysis.

## **Pillar 1 -- Time to Build**

The watch market correlation data that ContentRadar relies on takes years to develop. You need multiple seasonal cycles of keyword demand data across multiple market pairs to identify reliable geo-lag patterns, validate regulatory lag sequences, and build life event trigger models with statistical confidence. A competitor starting today cannot replicate this in 12 months.

## **Pillar 2 -- Market Specificity**

ContentRadar's value is not in generic keyword data -- Google Trends, DataForSEO, and Semrush all provide that. Its value is in knowing which keywords are relevant for a specific trade in a specific small Canadian market in a specific season. That specificity requires the watch market infrastructure, which requires the data history.

## **Pillar 3 -- The Diagnostic Integration**

ContentRadar is embedded in the diagnostic as the demand side of the content gap calculation. This means every diagnostic run reinforces the ContentRadar value proposition -- not as an add-on feature but as the core reason the diagnostic produces a personalised rather than generic revenue gap. Competitors cannot replicate the diagnostic without replicating ContentRadar.

## **Pillar 4 -- The Content Publishing Loop**

ContentRadar does not just identify gaps -- it closes them through the Blog, FAQs, and AI SEO modules. Every piece of content published through those modules is a direct output of the ContentRadar signal, indexed on the client's ClearSky website, building the organic position that makes the next diagnostic result better. The loop compounds over time in a way that generic keyword tools cannot replicate.

| ContentRadar is not a feature. It is the engine that makes ClearSky a compounding growth platform rather than a one-time optimisation service. Every content piece published based on a ContentRadar signal improves the client's diagnostic score. A better diagnostic score means a lower revenue gap. A lower revenue gap means the client can see their progress in real terms. This is what locks the client in -- not a contract, but measurable results. |
