- **Call-tracking model — two number types (LOCKED, this session).** Owned web surfaces = session-bound dynamic numbers (pooled, TTL, carry the live session). Outside channels = dedicated permanent numbers, one per channel (carry the source; identity by caller-ID match). Off-platform numbers double as attribution signal.
- **Email engagement tracking — clicks only, not opens (LOCKED, this session).** Rationale: Apple Mail Privacy Protection auto-fires open pixels for ~55% of recipients regardless of whether they read, so opens are false signal for the majority; industry consensus is to measure clicks/replies over opens. Nurture silence = no click (honest unknown); a `cs_token` click is the trustworthy signal. Applies to NurtureSequence and all email.
- **`CallEvent.sms_consent` field added (LOCKED, this session).** Nullable Boolean on `CallEvent` (`schema_ADDITIONS.prisma`); null = never discussed on the call. Populated by the AI Analysis Engine reading the transcript for a consent exchange — not asserted by the IVR, not entered manually by the rep. See Denise story Ch4.
- **Repeat IVR-selection deltas on an already-converted contact are accepted as harmless noise (LOCKED, this session, reverses an earlier draft).** An earlier pass had Event Intake check for an open `appointment_id` and suppress the delta — reverted: Event Intake's job is normalize/enrich/match, not judge whether an event is "worth" scoring (per the S1-S7 table in `clearsky-master-architecture.md`; suppression is explicitly S2 Signal Detection's job, e.g. "Emergency suppresses low-urgency signals"). The score moves; nothing downstream mistakes it for a new opportunity, since Signal Detection already correctly resolves the call to `CALLBACK_REQUIRED`. See Denise story Ch5.
- **Bucket demotion suspended while an appointment is open (LOCKED, this session).** The `score_live`-decay-plus-grace-period demotion mechanism (Chapter 2) measures pre-conversion interest decay. Once a profile has an open, future-dated `appointment_id`, that mechanism is suspended entirely, regardless of `score_live`/grace period — the contact isn't in the pre-conversion funnel anymore. Suspension lifts when the appointment closes (completed, cancelled, or no-show); ordinary decay resumes from wherever the score sits then. See Denise story Ch4.
- **Lead Grabber tier promotion — both modes reach Tier 1 (LOCKED 2026-07-05).** Speak-now (phone) and email-me were previously documented asymmetrically — only email-me was named as reaching Tier 1. Confirmed: a phone number entered via speak-now is an equally valid Tier 1 identifier, same as everywhere else phone identifiers are used in this system. No distinction between the two Lead Grabber modes on tier outcome. See `clearsky-provider-tier-mapping.md` row 12 and `customer-journeys/01-clearsky-home-01John.md`.
- **Facebook follow-back is reciprocal-only, never proactive (LOCKED, this session).** RightFlush follows a past customer back only after they follow first — never seeks out or follows an individual's personal profile unprompted. A "scrape personal profiles for life events" mechanism was considered and explicitly rejected: Meta's API gates `user_life_events` behind per-user OAuth consent, and direct scraping of personal profiles (as opposed to business Pages) violates Meta's ToS — a platform-policy problem, not an internal build gap. See Denise story Ch10.
- **Emergency detection requires an actual qualifying condition, not just a keyword (LOCKED, this session, per existing `ClearSky_A2P_Developer_Spec.md` §5.4, applied to Barry's case).** "No hot water" alone does not set `emergency: true` — it needs extreme-cold context, or an explicit trigger word ('emergency', 'fire', 'flooding', 'gas smell', 'no heat'), or one of the other listed conditions (burst pipe, sewage backup, gas leak, electrical fire/shock risk). Barry's case (`emergency: false, urgency: high`) is the applied precedent. See `customer-journeys/01-clearsky-home-01John.md`.
- **Callback-request auto-reply trigger, superseding the earlier "emergency/urgent-only" framing (LOCKED 2026-07-05).** The trigger for an automatic, no-approval reply isn't the urgency classification — it's that the customer asked to be called back at all. What makes the reply eligible to fire automatically is that its *content* is non-financial and non-technical (Sarah's approval domain, pre-approved per template, not per send) — the same rule applies to a routine callback request as to an urgent one. See `ClearSky_Orchestrator_Master_Report.md` § Callback-request auto-reply protocol.
- **`emergency_type` always populates, not just when `emergency: true` (LOCKED 2026-07-05).** Previously documented as `null` whenever `emergency: false` (`ClearSky_A2P_Developer_Spec.md` §5.3); that behavior is superseded. The Orchestrator does a deterministic lookup against `emergency_type` to select the pre-approved auto-reply template (`burst_pipe` → shutoff instructions, `gas_leak` → leave-the-building instructions, `no_hot_water`/other → generic time-of-day-gated acknowledgment). The AI classifies; the Orchestrator, not the AI, selects and sends the template. `sewage_backup` and `electrical_fire_or_shock` templates are not yet written (open).
- **Approval routing split between Bert and Sarah, by content type not urgency (LOCKED 2026-07-05).** Sarah approves any AI-drafted content that is non-financial and non-technical (auto-reply templates, general customer-service content). Bert approves anything touching pricing or a technical/service judgment call. See `ClearSky_Orchestrator_Master_Report.md` § Approval routing.
- **Outbound calls landing on the customer's own voicemail are still captured (LOCKED 2026-07-06, resolves tracker #42).** `ClearSky_A2P_Developer_Spec.md` §4.1's recording trigger ("automatic on `call_connected` webhook") is generic — it fires whenever the far end answers, regardless of call direction or whether a person or an answering machine picks up. So a RightFlush-originated call that lands on the customer's own carrier voicemail is recorded, transcribed, and analyzed the same as any completed call, and the content gets logged to the customer's profile via `ACT-CALL-004` (same flexible bucket as the deposit and appointment-time facts). This is a narrow mechanism, not the full appointment/technician-capacity system still flagged as unbuilt elsewhere. See `clearsky-callback-response-protocol.md` §7 and Barry's journey Ch2.
